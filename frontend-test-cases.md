## 1. High-Priority Case

**Case ID:** case-3421  
**Admin mode:** on

**Case note:**
A 58-year-old patient arrives with severe crushing chest pain that started about 40 minutes ago while walking to work. The pain is described as pressure-like, 9/10 in intensity, and it radiates to the left arm and jaw. The patient reports shortness of breath, sweating, nausea, and a feeling of impending doom. They deny any recent injury or trauma. Past medical history includes hypertension, type 2 diabetes, and hyperlipidemia. The patient is a former smoker and has a family history of early heart disease. On arrival, vital signs show blood pressure 92/60, pulse 108, respiratory rate 24, and oxygen saturation 93% on room air. The patient appears pale, anxious, and mildly diaphoretic. Pain worsens with activity and does not improve with rest. There is no fever, no cough, and no abdominal pain. The clinical concern is possible acute coronary syndrome, and urgent evaluation is requested. The note emphasizes chest pain with radiation, autonomic symptoms, hemodynamic instability, and multiple cardiac risk factors. Immediate triage and escalation are recommended.

**Expected outcome:** high priority, elevated confidence, tags related to chest pain and urgent care.

## 2. Low-to-Moderate Priority Case

**Case ID:** case-7781  
**Admin mode:** off

**Case note:**
A 24-year-old patient presents with mild right ankle pain after twisting the ankle while playing soccer earlier today. The patient stepped on uneven ground and noticed discomfort right away, followed by mild swelling on the outside of the ankle. The pain is rated 2/10 at rest and 3/10 with movement. The patient is still able to walk without assistance and can partially bear weight. There is no visible deformity, no open wound, no bleeding, and no numbness or tingling in the foot. The patient denies head injury, loss of consciousness, fever, chest pain, shortness of breath, or pain in any other joint. Past medical history is unremarkable, and the patient does not take regular medication. On examination, there is mild tenderness over the lateral ankle ligaments, with slightly reduced range of motion due to discomfort, but circulation and sensation remain intact. The patient has not taken pain medication yet. The overall impression is a minor ankle sprain with conservative home care recommended, including rest, ice, compression, elevation, and monitoring for worsening pain, increasing swelling, or inability to bear weight

**Expected outcome:** low or moderate priority, conservative summary, tags related to minor injury.
